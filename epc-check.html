EPC Related Information

What is an EPC?

An Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) is a mandatory document that rates your building's energy efficiency from A (most efficient) to G (least efficient). It provides crucial data about energy consumption and recommendations for improvements, helping building owners reduce operational costs while meeting legal requirements.

Why is it Required in South Africa?

South Africa's energy crisis and commitment to reducing carbon emissions have made EPCs legally mandatory. Buildings consume a large portion of the country's electricity. The EPC requirement ensures reduced strain on the national grid, lower operational costs, transparency for tenants and buyers, and alignment with international sustainability standards.

Brief History of EPC Requirements

2020
Draft regulations published following years of load-shedding and energy concerns
December 2022
Regulations gazetted, giving building owners 12-month grace period
December 2023
Grace period ended - enforcement begins
7 December 2025
Active enforcement starts with penalties for non-compliance including fines up to R5 million or 5 years imprisonment for false declarations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the EPC process take?

Typically 5-10 business days from initial assessment to certificate issuance.

What documents do I need?

Building plans, electricity bills, and HVAC system details are the primary requirements.

How long is an EPC valid?

10 years or until major renovations are undertaken, whichever comes first.
